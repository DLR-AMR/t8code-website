Enabling geometry-based h- and p-refinement

With version 1.0.0 we introduce curved cell geometries. With these geometries you are able to link features (edges and faces) of cells directly to OpenCASCADE Technology geometries (curves and surfaces). As you can see on the right, the linkage enables t8code to refine the cells along the provided geometry and even points evaluated inside an element are located with respect to the provided geometry.

Additionaly, you can use the mesh generator Gmsh, to create parametric mesh files. If you load these mesh files along with the meshed geometry, the cells get automatically linked to the geometry. This results in optimal h- and p-refinement along the geometries.

Take a look at our three publications for additional information.
Sandro Elsweijer, Evaluation and generic application scenarios for curved hexahedral adaptive mesh refinement, Masters thesis at the UAS Bonn-Rhein-Sieg, 2022, Full text available
Sandro Elsweijer and Johannes Holke and Jan Kleinert and Dirk Reith, Constructing a Volume Geometry Map for Hexahedra with Curved Boundary Geometries, SIAM International Meshing Roundtable Workshop 2022, 2022, Preprint available
Sandro Elsweijer, Curved Domain Adaptive Mesh Refinement with Hexahedra, Project at the UAS Bonn-Rhein-Sieg, 2021, Full text available
